Kael scrambled to his feet, palms burning. He thrust his hands outward, channeling the Weave as he'd been taught. Golden threads of light should have burst from his fingertips, weaving into a protective dome.

Instead, gray ash dribbled from his skin.

"Kael!" Elara grabbed his arm, her eyes wide. "The fountain—look!"

At the chamber's center, the Grand Font should have blazed with sapphire radiance. It had glowed for three centuries, feeding the Academy's endless hunger for mana. Now it sputtered like a dying candle, its light sinking into darkness.

The failing wasn't gradual anymore.

It was sudden, violent, and hungry.

A second-year named Marcus tried to cast a simple wind buffer. His spell imploded. The backlash hurled him across the room, body striking the stone wall with a wet crunch. He didn't get up.

"Everyone out!" Vane's robes whipped as he conjured a failing shield. His silver beard was smoking. "Evacuate the Spire! This is not a drill!"

Kael didn't run.

He couldn't.

Because in the depths of the dimming Font, something moved. Something vast and scaled and impossibly ancient. A single eye opened within the crystal waters—golden, slitted, fixing directly on Kael.

Aurelion.

The dragon spoke without sound, his voice echoing directly in Kael's skull:

*The final thread snaps at moonrise. Come to the Void, or watch the world go dark.*

Then the eye closed. The Font went black.

And the Academy began to fall.

"Help in the infirmary," Kael lied, keeping his voice steady. "I have healing certification."

Hale's eyes narrowed. She opened her mouth to demand proof.

The floor buckled.

Down three flights. Past the Alchemy labs where something was screaming in seventeen voices. Through the storage vaults where animate brooms had gone mad, attacking their shelves in suicidal frenzy.

Or dragons.
