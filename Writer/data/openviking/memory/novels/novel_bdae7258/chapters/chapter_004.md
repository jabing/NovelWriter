The crystalline fist caught Kael across the jaw before he could raise his ward.

Pain exploded through his skull. He tasted copper and ozone.

The construct towered above him—eight feet of jagged violet crystal animated by trapped lightning. Stolen magic. He could feel it humming against his skin, wrong and hungry.

"Move!" Lyra's voice cut through the thunder.

Kael rolled. The construct's blade-arm shattered the stone where his head had been.

Sparks danced across the spiral staircase. They were descending into the Crystal Spire's foundation, and every step cost blood.

"There's too many," Lyra gasped. She hurled a burst of emerald flame at a second construct. The spell splashed harmlessly against its chest, absorbed into the crystalline lattice. "They're eating our magic!"

Kael scrambled to his feet. His own power felt threadbare, fraying at the edges. The Fade was accelerating.

Three more constructs emerged from the walls, their bodies translucent prisons for screaming spells. Abandoned sorceries. Forgotten enchantments. All harvested by the Archons and weaponized.

"We can't fight them conventionally," Kael said. He wiped blood from his chin. "They're batteries. We need to break the cores."

"The cores are buried under three feet of enchanted diamond," Lyra snapped. She ducked as a whip of solidified starlight cracked overhead. "Any other ideas?"

Kael stared at the lead construct. Through its chest, he saw a pulsing node. A heart of compressed magic.

"Mine," he said.

He sprinted forward.

The construct swung. Kael dropped to his knees, sliding across the wet stone. Ice formed beneath his boots—his last reserves of power, barely enough to slick the ground.

He slid between the construct's legs.

Kael slammed his palm against the crystal heart. Instead of pushing outward, he pulled.

He yanked every thread of his fading magic into himself, creating a vacuum.

The construct shrieked. The stolen spells within it shivered, then poured into Kael like water down a drain.

Agony. Ecstasy.

The construct crumbled to dust.

Kael collapsed, convulsing. Foreign memories flooded his mind—a baker's love spell, a soldier's shield charm, a child's night-light enchantment. All stolen. All dying.

"Kael!" Lyra caught him. Her hands burned hot against his freezing skin. "What did you do?"

"Absorbed it," he choked out. "Temporary. Won't last."

He forced himself upright. The other constructs hesitated, their gemstone eyes flickering with something almost like fear.

"Keep moving," Kael said. "Before they adapt."

They ran.

***

The spiral ended in a cathedral of crystal.

Kael stumbled through the archway and froze. Lyra collided with his back.

"Oh," she whispered. "Oh, no."

The chamber stretched into impossible distance, a cavern lined with pillars of transparent stone. Within each pillar, a figure floated. Robed. Silent. Twisted in eternal agony.

Twelve pillars. Twelve prisoners.

Kael walked forward, drawn by a resonance in his blood. The first pillar contained a woman with his mother's eyes. The second, a man with his father's jawline.

"The seals," Kael breathed. "They're not magical barriers. They're people."

"The original dissenters," Lyra said. She pressed her hand against a pillar, recoiling at the cold. "The ones who tried to stop the Archons three centuries ago. They didn't kill them. They turned them into... batteries."

Kael reached the eighth pillar. The face inside was unmistakable. Thane Vane. His great-grandfather, erased from family records, spoken of only in whispers.

The man's eyes were open. Aware.

"They're alive," Kael said. Horror hollowed out his chest. "They've been alive this whole time. Feeling everything."

A pulse rippled through the chamber. The twelve prisoners twitched simultaneously.

At the far end of the cathedral, a thirteenth pillar stood alone. It didn't contain a person.

It contained a golden light.

"The final seal," Lyra said. "Aurelion."

Kael approached. The light wasn't a prison. It was a doorway.

He placed his hand against the crystal.

The world dissolved.

***

Kael stood in an infinite sky.

No ground. No ceiling. Only drifting clouds of liquid gold and the overwhelming presence of something ancient watching him.

"You are small," said a voice like mountains grinding together. "And you are loud."

"Aurelion," Kael said. He didn't bow. He didn't dare.

The dragon coalesced from the golden mist. Not a physical form—an impression of vastness. Wings that could eclipse suns. Eyes that held centuries.

"You carry my descendants' blood," Aurelion said. "And you carry their stubbornness."

"I came to free you," Kael said. "The Archons are harvesting magic. The world is dying. I need your power to stop them."

Aurelion laughed. It sounded like avalanches.

"Physical freedom is a cage of another sort," the dragon said. "My body is destroyed. Scattered. The Archons consumed it centuries ago to fuel their immortality."

Kael's heart sank. "Then there's nothing? No way to restore the magic?"

"There is the Soul-Bond."

The golden mist thickened, pressing against Kael's skin.

"I am consciousness without form," Aurelion said. "You are form with failing power. Together, we could restore the natural flow. Re-seed the world with magic. But it requires a vessel. A guide. A living mind willing to merge with something incomprehensible."

"You want to possess me," Kael said.

"I want to partner with you," Aurelion corrected. "You would not disappear. You would become... more. The boundary between us would thin. You would carry the draconic consciousness within your mortal frame. The power would flow through you, healing the Fade."

"And if I refuse?"

"Then the harvest completes. The Archons ascend. Magic dies forever."

Kael looked down at his hands. They were already translucent at the edges, flickering with the stolen spells he'd absorbed.

"I need time," Kael said.

"You have seconds."

The sky shattered.

***

Kael crashed back into his body. Lyra was shaking him, shouting his name.

"They're coming," she said. "Kael, they're coming!"

The chamber shook. Dust rained from the ceiling.

Thirteen figures descended through the crystal roof.

They didn't fall. They flowed like ink through water.

The Archons.

Their robes were pristine white, but their exposed skin betrayed their true nature. Scales shimmered beneath their cheeks. Their eyes were vertical slits of molten gold. Dragon-human hybrids, kept immortal by centuries of stolen power.

At their center floated Malachar.

His face was still handsome, still cruel, but now Kael saw the horns beneath his hairline. The tail whipping behind him.

"Kael Vane," Malachar said. His voice carried harmonics that made Kael's teeth ache. "You've found our little power source."

Kael stepped in front of Lyra. "You've been feeding on them. On my family. On Aurelion."

"Survival requires sacrifice," Malachar said. He touched down lightly, his clawed feet clicking on stone. "The Fade is natural. Magic is finite. We simply... manage the remainder."

"By hoarding it. By letting the world die."

"We are preserving it!" Malachar's eyes flashed. "Without us, magic would have vanished two centuries ago. We store it. We ration it. We are the gardeners of the arcane."

"You're parasites," Lyra spat.

Malachar smiled. "Perhaps. But we are eternal parasites."

He gestured. The other twelve Archons spread out, forming a circle. Their hands began to glow with ritual light.

"Above us," Malachar said, "the final harvest begins. The last dregs of natural magic will be drawn into the Crystal Spire. Then we ascend. We become true gods."

He turned to Kael. His expression softened into something almost paternal.

"But you, Kael. You have potential. You absorbed the construct's power. You bear the dragon's mark. You could be the Fourteenth."

Kael blinked. "What?"

"Join us," Malachar said. He extended a hand. Scales glittered on his wrist. "Kill Aurelion's consciousness. Destroy the Soul-Bond option. Take your place among us. Immortality. Power. The woman you love preserved beside you for eternity."

He looked at Lyra. "She would live, Kael. If you accept. If you refuse, she dies here. Now."

The ritual circle above them flared. The twelve prisoner-batteries screamed in unison.

"Choose," Malachar said. "The dragon. Or us."

Kael looked at the thirteenth pillar. The golden light pulsed steadily. Waiting.

He looked at Lyra. Her eyes were wide, terrified, but she shook her head. Don't, she mouthed.

"Kael," Malachar said. "Decide."

A explosion rocked the chamber's entrance.

Stone shattered. Dust billowed.

A figure stumbled through, bleeding from a dozen wounds, clutching a broken staff.

"Sylas?" Kael breathed.

The archivist looked worse than death. His robes were scorched. His left arm hung useless.

"I found the circle," Sylas wheezed. "The ritual. I know how to break it."

Malachar snarled. "Kill him."

One of the Archons lashed out. A blade of compressed shadow shot toward Sylas.

Kael moved. He hurled himself into the path.

But Sylas was faster.

The archivist shoved Kael aside and took the blade through his chest.

"Sylas!"

"No!" Sylas screamed. Not in pain. In defiance.

He slammed his broken staff onto the floor. The crystal cracked.

Light erupted from the fissures. Not golden. Not white. Pure, wild, natural magic.

"The circle breaks!" Sylas shouted. Blood poured from his mouth. "Complete the Bond, you fool! Now!"

The ritual above shuddered. The Archons screamed as their connection to the harvest snapped.

Malachar lunged for Kael.

Kael turned and ran.

He didn't hesitate. He didn't doubt.

He plunged his hands into the golden light of the thirteenth pillar.

"Aurelion!" Kael shouted. "I accept!"

The Soul-Bond ignited.

Kael's scream harmonized with the dragon's roar. His skin began to glow from within, golden veins spreading across his body like molten wire.

Malachar's claws raked his back, but the blow glanced off scales that hadn't been there a moment before.

Kael felt his mind expanding. Aurelion poured into him—not a possession, but a merging. Ancient memories collided with his own. Flight above primordial clouds. The taste of starfire. The weight of centuries.

He felt the magic of the world rushing toward him, no longer fading but flowing, drawn to the new vessel like iron to a magnet.

"Stop him!" Malachar shrieked.

But the golden light was spreading, consuming the chamber, transforming the crystal into living gold.

Kael turned to face the Archons. His eyes burned with draconic fire.

Lyra reached for him, but her hand passed through his shoulder. He was becoming less solid, more luminous, existing in multiple states at once.

"Kael?" she whispered. "What's happening?"

Kael tried to speak, but his voice came out in chords of thunder and melody.

The Bond wasn't complete.

It was hungry.

And as the light consumed him, Kael realized with perfect, terrible clarity that he wasn't just merging with Aurelion.

He was becoming the new source of all magic.

Whether he survived the process... was no longer certain.
