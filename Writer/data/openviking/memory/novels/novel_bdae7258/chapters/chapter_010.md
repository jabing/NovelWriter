The blue light flickered.

The stone fell.

Outside, the Academy's protective wards were visible for the first time in decades. Massive hexagonal shields shimmered against the sky, pulsing irregularly. With each flicker, sections of the golden lattice vanished.

Then didn't return.

"The Seventh Seal is breaking," someone whispered.

"Quiet!" A new voice boomed.

High Chancellor Malachar descended the spiral stairs, his crimson robes sweeping behind him like spilled blood. His silver eyes surveyed the chamber with predatory precision. When he looked at Kael, a smile played at his lips.

"An interesting display, Mr. Vane," Malachar said. "Or should I say... a revealing one?"

Kael straightened, dusting stone fragments from his tunic. "The spell was perfect. I felt the framework hold."

"Did you?" Malachar stepped closer. The air grew heavy, scented with ozone and something darker. "Or did you finally prove what I've suspected? That the Vane bloodline has lost its connection to the Source?"

Lyra moved between them. "With respect, Chancellor, three other students failed elementary conjurations this morning. This isn't about bloodlines."

Malachar's gaze slid to her. For a moment, his composure cracked—irritation flashing like lightning. Then it was gone, replaced by calculated warmth.

"Miss Ashford. Ever the advocate for lost causes." He gestured to the broken stone. "Clean this up. Both of you. Then report to my office. The Academy must address this... epidemic."

He turned, but not before Kael saw the satisfaction in his eyes. Malachar wasn't surprised by the fading. He'd expected it.

That realization settled in Kael's gut like ice.
