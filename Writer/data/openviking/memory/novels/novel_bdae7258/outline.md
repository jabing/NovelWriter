Chapter 1: The Hollow Mountain
- Kael, a junior archivist mage with weak but precise magical sensitivity, is sent to catalog the forbidden Caverns of Echoing Stone—a punishment for questioning his superiors
- While mapping a collapsed tunnel, he discovers glyphs that predate human magic, pulsing with a rhythm like a heartbeat
- Following the pulse, he finds Aurelion: not a raging beast, but an ancient dragon imprisoned within a crystalline lattice of stolen magic, visibly emaciated despite its size
- The dragon opens one eye and speaks directly into Kael's mind, revealing that the "ley-lines" powering the Mage Council's civilization are actually siphons draining the dragon's life-force
- Kael flees in terror, but finds his own magic has changed—his spells now taste of ash, and he realizes he's been unknowingly drawing from the dragon's stolen essence his entire life

Chapter 2: The Sundered History
- Unable to sleep, Kael returns secretly to the caves and begins learning Aurelion's true history: five centuries ago, dragons and mages lived in symbiosis, sharing mana freely
- The first Archmages betrayed this pact, imprisoning Aurelion to create "wells" of concentrated magic that granted them immortality and dominion
- Kael's mentor, Archivist Sylas, confronts him about his secret research; Kael tries to explain, but Sylas warns him that "dragon-madness" is a known heresy punishable by death
- Investigating the library archives with new eyes, Kael finds evidence of the betrayal—redacted histories where the word "partner" was replaced with "beast," and missing records of thirteen mages who refused the new system
- Kael discovers he bears the Dragon-Mark, a faint scaling pattern on his palm indicating ancient bloodline ties to the original dragon-pact, explaining why he alone can hear Aurelion's voice

Chapter 3: The Breaking Point
- The Council announces plans to "harvest the Deep Source" completely—a ceremony that will kill Aurelion and transfer his remaining power to the Archons, making them gods
- Aurelion reveals the final secret: he is not merely a prisoner, but the physical heart of magic itself; if he dies, magic leaves the world forever, leaving it a dead husk
- Kael must choose: warn the Council and preserve his comfortable life (but doom the future), or break the seven seals binding Aurelion (which will unleash chaotic, uncontrolled magic and destroy the capital city built atop the wells)
- Kael chooses rebellion. He attempts to break the first seal in the Aqueduct Sanctum, but Sylas discovers him and they duel; Kael wins but refuses to kill his mentor, instead showing him the Mark
- As Sylas realizes the truth, the Council's enforcers arrive—Kael escapes wounded, but Sylas is captured, buying Kael time to reach the Heart-Chamber

Chapter 4: The Convergence
- Kael descends into the Crystal Spire's foundation, fighting through constructs made of crystallized stolen magic, while above, the Archons begin the final harvest ritual
- He discovers the other twelve seals are actually the bodies of the original dissenting mages, preserved in agony as living batteries—his own ancestors among them
- Breaking the final seal, Kael doesn't free Aurelion physically; instead, the dragon offers a Soul-Bond, a merging of consciousness that will restore the natural flow of magic but require a living vessel to guide it
- The Archons descend, revealing their true forms—twisted dragon-human hybrids kept immortal by the stolen power—and offer Kael the fourteenth seat among them if he kills Aurelion
- Sylas escapes and arrives to help, but is mortally wounded protecting Kael; with his dying breath, he breaks the ritual circle, allowing Kael to complete the Bond

Chapter 5: The Unbinding
- Kael and Aurelion merge, becoming the Dragon-Soul: neither human nor dragon, but a new entity of flowing, living magic that cannot be caged
- Together, they shatter the wells; the explosion of returning magic doesn't destroy the city, but transforms it—stone becomes living crystal, dead gardens bloom with impossible colors, and the sky splits with auroras of raw power
- The Archons, deprived of their stolen immortality, crumble to dust as centuries catch up with them in seconds
