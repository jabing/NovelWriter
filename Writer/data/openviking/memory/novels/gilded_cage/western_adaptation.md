# The Gilded Cage - Western Adaptation

## Character Bible

### Main Characters

| Role | Name | Age Range | Description |
|------|------|-----------|-------------|
| Protagonist | **Clara Whitmore** | 14-18 | Small-town girl, piano prodigy, observant but introverted. Lives with her mother (single parent, music teacher). Dark hair, striking gray eyes that "see too much." |
| Love Interest | **Julian Ashford** | 15-19 | Mysterious transfer student. Secretly the illegitimate son of the Ashford dynasty. Charming but haunted. Dark hair, deep brown eyes, a faint scar on his left wrist. |
| Antagonist | **Helena Van Der Berg** | 45 | Elegant society matron, philanthropist, art patron. Beautiful, cultured, terrifying. The gatekeeper who "discovers" talented young things. |
| Supporting | **Serena Hale** | 15-19 | Clara's age, already deep in the circle. Golden girl exterior, broken interior. Becomes Clara's only true ally. |
| Supporting | **Nathaniel Ashford** | 50s | Julian's father. Old money, old sins. Owns half of Connecticut. |
| Supporting | **Margaret Whitmore** | 40s | Clara's mother. Public school music teacher. Loving but unaware of the world her daughter is entering. |

### Minor Characters
- **Dashiell Crane** - Serena's "patron," Wall Street predator
- **Vivian Chen** - Another girl in the circle, didn't survive
- **Detective Morrison** - Investigates in Act Three
- **Dr. Rebecca Hart** - Clara's therapist in recovery

## Settings

### Act One: Small-Town America
- **Maple Falls, Connecticut** - Fictional small town, 2 hours from NYC
- **Maple Falls High School** - Public school, old building, music room with a vintage Steinway
- **Clara's apartment** - Above the music store her mother manages
- **The gazebo in Memorial Park** - Where Clara and Julian have their first kiss

### Act Two: The Gilded World
- **Ashford Estate, Greenwich** - Historic mansion, 40 rooms, private beach
- **The Van Der Berg Mansion, Upper East Side** - Townhouse where "gatherings" happen
- **St. Jude's Academy** - Elite private school Clara transfers to
- **The Hamptons house** - Summer "parties"
- **Private island in Maine** - Where the deepest darkness happens

### Act Three: The Aftermath
- **Psychiatric facility** - Where Clara recovers
- **Courtroom** - The trials
- **New apartment in Boston** - Clara's fresh start

## Symbolism (Retained)

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **The Piano** | Talent as both gift and curse; the instrument of her entry into that world |
| **Butterflies** | Beauty, transformation, fragility; pinned specimens in Helena's collection |
| **Mirrors** | The false selves; Clara's fractured identity |
| **The Ocean** | The Ashford estate's private beach; drowning imagery |
| **Gold/Gilding** | Surface beauty hiding rot; the cage itself |
| **Gardens** | Helena's prize-winning roses; trimmed, controlled, beautiful above rotting soil |

## Tone & Style (Western YA/NA)

### Comparable Works
- **My Dark Vanessa** (Kate Elizabeth Russell) - The psychological complexity
- **The Secret History** (Donna Tartt) - Elite circles, dark secrets
- **Eleanor & Park** (Rainbow Rowell) - The first love sweetness (Act One)
- **A Little Life** (Hanya Yanagihara) - The depths of trauma (Act Three)
- **You** (Caroline Kepnes) - Psychological intensity

### Writing Approach
- First person, Clara's POV
- Present tense for immediacy (optional)
- Lyrical but accessible
- Psychological depth over explicit content
- Contrast: Innocent voice slowly understanding horror

## Plot Structure (350 Chapters)

### Act One: The Golden Summer (Chapters 1-100)
- Small-town romance with Julian
- The piano competition that changes everything
- The first invitation to "The Estate"
- Summer intensifies, reality blurs
- Ends: Julian is taken away, Clara is alone

### Act Two: The Gilded Cage (Chapters 101-250)
- Transfer to St. Jude's Academy
- Initiation into "The Circle"
- The parties, the men, the silence
- Serena's friendship and warning
- Serena's death (suicide or "accident")
- Clara's attempted escape and punishment
- The island

### Act Three: Shattered Glass (Chapters 251-350)
- The investigation begins
- Clara as witness
- The trial(s)
- The aftermath - therapy, recovery
- Writing her story
- Ending: Not happy, but alive. Healing.
